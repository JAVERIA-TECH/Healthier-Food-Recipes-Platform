Nihari

Breakfast

A rich, slow-cooked beef stew infused with spices, traditionally enjoyed in Lahore and Karachi.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef shank (with bone)
  • 1 cup oil or ghee
  • 1 large onion (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp nihari masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tbsp wheat flour
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Garnish: Ginger slices, green chilies, lemon, coriander

Instructions

  1. Heat oil/ghee in a large pot. Add onions and fry until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger garlic paste and sauté for a minute.
  3. Add beef and cook until it changes color.
  4. Add all spices and cook for 10-15 mins, stirring frequently.
  5. Add enough water to cover the meat. Cook on low flame for 4-6 hours until tender.
  6. Mix wheat flour in water, add to the curry to thicken.
  7. Garnish and serve hot with naan or kulcha.

Halwa Puri with Chana & Aloo Tarkari

Breakfast

A classic breakfast combo, usually enjoyed on Sundays, consisting of deep-fried bread (puri), sweet halwa made from semolina, chickpeas (chana), and spiced potatoes (aloo tarkari).

Ingredients for Puri

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji)
  • Salt & water (to knead)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Ingredients for Halwa

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • Optional: Cardamom, raisins, almonds

Instructions

  1. Puri: Knead dough, rest for a bit, roll into small discs, and deep fry until they puff up.
  2. Halwa: Roast semolina in ghee, add sugar and water, and cook until it becomes thick.
  3. Chana: Fry onions, add tomatoes and spices, then add chickpeas and let it simmer.
  4. Aloo Tarkari: Sauté onions, tomatoes, and spices, then add potatoes and cook for 10 minutes.

Payee (Trotters Curry)

Breakfast

A rich and flavorful stew made with cow or goat trotters, full of gelatin, a special dish from Lahore.

Ingredients

  • 4 cleaned cow/goat trotters
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • red chili, turmeric, coriander, garam masala, salt
  • 1/2 cup wheat flour (to thicken the curry)
  • 6-8 cups water
  • Oil or ghee for cooking

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot and fry onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger garlic paste and spices. Cook until the oil separates.
  3. Add trotters, stir for 10 minutes.
  4. Add water, cover, and let it cook slowly for 6-8 hours (or pressure cook for 1-2 hours).
  5. Mix flour with water and add it to thicken the curry. Serve hot.

Desi Omelette

Breakfast

A spicy omelette made with veggies and herbs, commonly eaten for breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Salt, chili flakes

Instructions

  1. Beat eggs and add all ingredients.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, pour the egg mixture.
  3. Cook both sides until golden.
  4. Serve with paratha or toast.

Aloo ke Paratha

Breakfast

Flatbreads stuffed with spicy mashed potatoes, popular in Punjab.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 boiled potatoes
  • Spices: cumin, chili, salt, garam masala
  • Fresh coriander, green chili
  • Oil or ghee for frying

Instructions

  1. Make dough from flour and water.
  2. Mash potatoes with spices and herbs.
  3. Roll the dough, fill with the mashed potatoes, seal, and roll again.
  4. Cook on a tawa (griddle) with ghee until golden.

Anda Paratha Roll

Breakfast

A street-style roll filled with a spiced egg and served with chutneys.

Ingredients

  • 1 paratha (can use frozen or fresh)
  • 1 egg
  • Salt, pepper
  • Optional: Onion and green chutney

Instructions

  1. Cook paratha on a tawa (flat griddle).
  2. Beat the egg and season with salt and pepper, then pour it on a hot tawa.
  3. Place the paratha on top and press gently until the egg sticks.
  4. Roll it up with onions and chutney.

Biryani

Lunch

A festive, layered rice dish with spiced chicken or meat, saffron, and fried onions, often served at special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp biryani masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron or yellow food color
  • Oil and water as needed

Instructions

  1. Boil rice with salt until 70% cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Fry onions until golden, remove half for garnish.
  3. In same pot, add ginger-garlic paste, chicken, tomatoes, yogurt, spices. Cook until oil separates and chicken is done.
  4. In a large pot, layer rice and chicken curry. Sprinkle food color or saffron water, and fried onions.
  5. Cover tightly and steam (dum) for 20 minutes on low heat.
  6. Gently mix and serve.

Chicken Karahi

Lunch

A spicy and tomato-rich chicken curry made in a wok (karahi), popular across Pakistan.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (with bones)
  • 3 tomatoes (blanched and chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp crushed red chili
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • Green chilies and coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a karahi, add chicken and sauté for 5-6 minutes.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and spices, cook for another few minutes.
  3. Add tomatoes, cover, and cook until soft.
  4. Uncover and cook until oil separates and chicken is tender.
  5. Garnish with green chilies and coriander.

Daal Chawal (Lentils & Rice)

Lunch

Comfort food made of simple yellow lentils (moong or masoor) served with plain rice and spicy tempering.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup moong/masoor daal
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee
  • For Tarka:
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 garlic cloves (sliced)
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 whole red chilies

Instructions

  1. Wash and boil lentils with turmeric and salt until soft.
  2. In a pan, heat oil/ghee for tarka. Fry onions, garlic, cumin, and chilies until golden.
  3. Pour tarka over cooked daal.
  4. Serve hot with plain boiled rice.

Sabzi Pulao (Mixed Vegetable Rice)

Lunch

A fragrant and colorful rice dish cooked with seasonal vegetables like potatoes, carrots, peas, and bell peppers.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 potato (cubed)
  • 1 carrot (sliced)
  • 1/2 cup green peas
  • 1/2 bell pepper (chopped)
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/4 cup oil

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak rice for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pot, add cumin seeds and sliced onions.
  3. Sauté until light brown, then add green chilies and tomato.
  4. Add all vegetables, salt, and spices. Cook for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Add 4 cups water and bring to a boil.
  6. Drain rice and add to the pot.
  7. Cook on medium heat until water is absorbed, then simmer on low (dum) for 10 minutes.
  8. Fluff rice and serve.

Bhindi Gosht (Okra & Meat)

Lunch

Bhindi Gosht is a flavorful dish made with tender mutton or beef and okra (bhindi), cooked in a spiced tomato-onion gravy.

Ingredients

  • 500g mutton or beef (bone-in)
  • 300g bhindi (okra), chopped
  • 2 onions, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup oil
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat half the oil and fry bhindi until lightly golden, then set aside.
  2. In a pot, heat remaining oil and fry cumin seeds.
  3. Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste, cook for a minute.
  5. Add meat and sauté until it changes color.
  6. Add tomatoes, chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  7. Add 2 cups water and pressure cook or simmer until meat is tender.
  8. Add fried bhindi and cook uncovered for 10 minutes.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve.

Yakhni Pulao (Beef/Mutton)

Dinner

A savory rice dish made with aromatic stock (yakhni) and tender meat, full of subtle spices.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef/mutton with bones
  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 bay leaf, 4 cloves, 4 black peppercorns, 2 green cardamoms, 1 stick cinnamon
  • Salt to taste
  • 6 cups water for stock
  • 1/4 cup oil

Instructions

  1. Boil meat with whole spices, salt, and ginger-garlic paste until tender. Strain and reserve yakhni.
  2. Heat oil and sauté sliced onion until golden.
  3. Add boiled meat and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add yakhni (equal to twice the rice volume), bring to a boil.
  5. Add soaked rice, cook until water reduces.
  6. Cover and simmer (dum) for 10 minutes.
  7. Serve with raita or salad.

Dahi Bhallay

Dinner

Soft lentil fritters soaked in spiced yogurt and topped with chutneys and chaat masala.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup mash daal (urad daal)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups thick yogurt (whisked)
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1 tsp chaat masala
  • Sweet tamarind chutney
  • Green chutney
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Soak daal for 4-6 hours and grind to a smooth batter.
  2. Add salt and baking soda, beat until fluffy.
  3. Deep fry spoonfuls until golden.
  4. Soak bhallay in warm water for 10 minutes, then squeeze water out.
  5. Place in a dish and pour over seasoned yogurt.
  6. Top with chutneys, cumin powder, and chaat masala.

Pakoray (My Favourite)

Dinner

Crispy fried fritters made with gram flour and vegetables like onions, potatoes, and spinach.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1 potato (thinly sliced)
  • 1/2 cup chopped spinach
  • 1 green chili, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Mix all ingredients to make a thick batter.
  2. Heat oil and drop small portions into oil.
  3. Fry until golden brown and crisp.
  4. Serve with green chutney or ketchup.

Gulab Jamun

Dessert

Soft, deep-fried milk-solid balls soaked in fragrant sugar syrup. A classic South Asian dessert.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Milk as needed
  • Oil/ghee for frying
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • Cardamom and rose water (optional)

Instructions

  1. Mix dry ingredients, add ghee and milk to form soft dough.
  2. Make smooth balls, no cracks.
  3. Fry on low heat until golden.
  4. Make syrup with sugar, water, cardamom.
  5. Add fried jamuns to warm syrup and soak for 2-3 hours.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Ras Malai

Dessert

Soft paneer patties soaked in sweetened, thickened milk flavored with cardamom and saffron.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice/vinegar
  • 1 liter milk for rabri
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Few strands saffron
  • Chopped pistachios for garnish

Instructions

  1. Boil 1 liter milk, add lemon juice to curdle it.
  2. Strain through muslin cloth to make paneer.
  3. Knead paneer until smooth, make small discs.
  4. Boil sugar syrup and cook discs for 15 minutes.
  5. For rabri: Boil milk until reduced by half, add sugar, cardamom, saffron.
  6. Soak cooked discs in rabri for 2 hours.
  7. Garnish with pistachios and serve chilled.

Sheer Khurma

Dessert

A festive vermicelli pudding made with milk, dates, and dry fruits, traditionally served on Eid.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup vermicelli
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 10-12 dates (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • Assorted nuts (almonds, pistachios, cashews)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee and lightly fry vermicelli until golden.
  2. Boil milk in a separate pot.
  3. Add fried vermicelli to milk and cook until soft.
  4. Add sugar, dates, and cardamom powder.
  5. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until thickened.
  6. Garnish with fried nuts and serve warm or chilled.

Zarda (Sweet Rice)

Dessert

A fragrant sweet rice dish colored yellow or orange, made with sugar, ghee, and dry fruits.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup ghee
  • 1/4 tsp yellow/orange food color
  • 10-12 almonds (sliced)
  • 10-12 raisins
  • 4-5 green cardamoms
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 4 cups water

Instructions

  1. Soak rice for 30 minutes, then boil until 70% cooked. Drain.
  2. Heat ghee in a pan, fry almonds and raisins. Remove.
  3. In same ghee, add cardamoms, food color, and 1/4 cup water.
  4. Add sugar and let it dissolve completely.
  5. Add rice and mix gently. Cover and cook on low heat for 15 minutes.
  6. Garnish with fried nuts and serve warm.

Shahi Tukray (Bread Pudding)

Dessert

A rich Mughlai dessert made with fried bread slices soaked in thickened, sweetened milk and garnished with nuts.

Ingredients

  • 6 slices white bread (crusts removed)
  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 tsp cardamom powder
  • Few strands saffron
  • Ghee for frying
  • Chopped pistachios and almonds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Cut bread into triangles and fry in ghee until golden. Set aside.
  2. Boil milk until reduced to half, stirring frequently.
  3. Add sugar, cardamom, and saffron. Cook until slightly thickened.
  4. Arrange fried bread in a dish and pour warm milk mixture over it.
  5. Let it soak for 1-2 hours.
  6. Garnish with nuts and serve chilled.
